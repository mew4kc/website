Demonstrating the Value of the Appomattox Trail


Title:

Demonstrating the Value of the Appomattox Trail


Date:

8-7-2020


Author(s):

Kyra Faison, Kyle Jacobs, Courtney Lawson, Jaida Robinson, Mousa Toure, Mychala Walker


Abstract:

Friends of the Lower Appomattox River (FOLAR) partnered with the Virginia State University DSPG team to show the value of the Appomattox River through economic and social means. The current trail runs through Chesterfield County, Dinwiddie County, Prince George County, Colonial Heights, Hopewell, and Petersburg.Our main area of focus was Petersburg, VA. When researching we broke all of the potential factors down into four main topics: Policy, Health, Transportation, and Economy. We found that while there is high access to parks and trails, crime and safety concerns cause low usage among residents. Low usage can result in limited spending from tourism and retail activity. Overall, we found that with policy and safety changes the Appomattox River trail has the potential to result in health and economic benefits for its surrounding areas.
